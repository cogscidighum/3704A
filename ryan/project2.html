Project 2: Stoicism as an Important and Practical Philosophy - A Reflection

by Ryan Lyster

The Importance of Stoicism

Stoicism, a philosophy emphasizing self-control, rationality, and virtue, advocates that our well-being is determined more by our inner attitudes and perceptions than by external events. This philosophy resonates deeply with modern cognitive science, which also recognizes how our perceptions shape our emotions and experiences. Personally, embracing a Stoic approach, enriched by insights from my time in cognitive science, has transformed the way I examine and reframe my thoughts. This shift allows me to respond to real-world situations with a more balanced and reasoned mindset, enhancing my emotional resilience and enabling me to navigate life’s challenges with greater clarity. Integrating Stoic wisdom with cognitive insights cultivates a mindful and proactive life approach, where reactions are shaped by deliberate thought and self-awareness, rather than dictated by circumstances. As Ryan Holiday aptly puts it, “Stoicism is a practical philosophy.”

Four Virtues

Central to Stoicism and its everyday application are the four cardinal virtues: Courage, Justice, Temperance, and Wisdom. Courage in Stoicism transcends physical challenges; it's about confronting cognitive and emotional obstacles with resilience—a concept I’ve found personally empowering when facing my own fears and uncertainties. Practicing Stoic courage allows for decisions unclouded by emotional biases. Justice in Stoicism, which extends to fairness in thoughts and actions, aligns with the cognitive science principle of social cognition—how we perceive and interact with others. This principle has helped me improve my social relationships and mental health by treating others with respect and empathy. Temperance, or self-regulation, a concept extensively explored in cognitive science, has taught me to manage impulses and desires, maintaining a balanced lifestyle. Finally, Wisdom, the virtue of sound judgment and knowing what is in your control and not in your control, parallels cognitive science’s exploration of decision-making and introspection. Cultivating Stoic wisdom has enhanced my ability to view situations from multiple angles, leading to more effective decision-making.

Objective

My objective in this reflection is to delve into a selection of Ryan Holiday's videos, examining each through the lens of the four Stoic virtues: Courage, Justice, Temperance, and Wisdom. Ryan Holiday stands out as a modern author and thinker, renowned for his contributions to the revival of Stoicism and its integration into today's lifestyle. The aim is to connect the content of each video to these virtues, exploring how they manifest in various contexts and teachings presented by Holiday. This critical examination aims to provide a deeper understanding of Stoicism's practicality in everyday life.Videos

7 Stoic Keys To Being A Great Leader | Ryan Holiday Speaks To The U.S. Military

Ryan Holiday addresses the U.S. Military on Stoicism's role in leadership, emphasizing control over internal responses amidst external challenges. He draws from Seneca's view of life as a battle and cites historical and modern Stoic figures in military leadership, like Marcus Aurelius and James Stockdale, to illustrate Stoicism's practicality in adversity. Holiday explores the moral aspect of courage, citing figures like Lieutenant Bradley Snyder and John Lewis, and intertwines Stoic philosophy with military values to highlight its relevance in resilience, ethical decision-making, and personal growth. Holiday's talk subtly incorporates the four Stoic virtues. Courage is evident in the emphasis on moral strength and resilience in leadership, aligning with Stoicism's focus on integrity and bravery in adversity. Justice is reflected in the ethical underpinnings of leadership decisions, resonating with Stoicism's emphasis on fairness and the greater good. Temperance is inferred from discussions on self-control and balance, particularly in high-pressure situations, highlighting the importance of rational decision-making. Wisdom is present in the emphasis on learning from history and applying this knowledge judiciously, a key aspect of Stoic thought. Holiday's practical, example-driven approach makes Stoic principles accessible and relevant, particularly to a military audience, demonstrating how these virtues can be applied in modern leadership scenarios for effective and ethical governance.

9 Stoic Secrets For Self-Mastery (From Experts)

This video delves into how Stoicism aids in achieving a longer, healthier life. He contrasts Stoicism's awareness of death with its emphasis on active pursuit of health, as exemplified by Marcus Aurelius and Seneca. Holiday underscores the idea that good health is crucial for effective living and counters Stoic principles to indulge in detrimental habits. He advocates for embracing challenges that test and strengthen both body and mind, like meditation and physical endurance exercises, to foster resilience and control. In this talk, Holiday intertwines Stoic virtues with aspects of health and well-being. Courage is seen in the proactive approach to health challenges and the mental strength required to sustain a healthy lifestyle. This reflects Stoicism's call for bravery not just in the face of external adversity but also in personal health and well-being. Justice is portrayed in the respect and care for one's own body, recognizing that personal health has broader social implications. By staying healthy, one can better serve and contribute to the community. Temperance is highlighted in the disciplined approach to managing desires and maintaining a balanced lifestyle, crucial for long-term health and vitality. Wisdom is apparent in the application of Stoic principles to health-related decisions, emphasizing the importance of self- awareness and informed choices for overall well-being. The discussion in this video offers a modern perspective on Stoicism, showing its practical application in maintaining holistic health. Holiday’s insights align with contemporary health psychology, emphasizing the interconnectedness of mental and physical health and the role of lifestyle choices in enhancing life quality. This reflection underscores Stoicism's relevance in offering actionable strategies for a balanced and fulfilling life.

8 Stoic Habits That Will Improve Your Life

Here, Holiday presents eight Stoic habits for a better life, starting with the importance of daily preparation and mental readiness. He advocates for walking as a means of mental clarity, consistency in actions for goal achievement, and community engagement for mutual enrichment. Holiday also emphasizes personal growth through challenging oneself, the value of cherishing relationships, the significance of reading for continuous learning, and the use of affirmations for resilience and positive thinking. Holiday's discussion of these habits aligns with the Stoic virtues. The habit of daily preparation reflects Wisdom, involving foresight and reflective thinking. Walking, as a balanced and moderate activity, embodies Temperance, promoting mental and physical harmony. Engaging with the community and valuing relationships resonate with Justice, emphasizing social bonds and ethical responsibilities. Challenging oneself and embracing personal growth are acts of Courage, demonstrating bravery in personal development and adaptability. In this video, Holiday's practical approach makes Stoicism relevant and applicable in daily life. His focus on these habits illustrates how Stoic virtues can be actively incorporated into daily routines, aiding personal growth and enhancing life quality. This reflection shows Stoicism's practicality in modern living, offering a structured approach to cultivating a balanced and fulfilling life.

8 Stoic Don'ts For A Better Life

In this video, Holiday presents a series of Stoic 'don'ts' for a fulfilling life. He advises against fearing change, emphasizing its potential for positive outcomes. Holiday recommends not getting bogged down by trivial matters and valuing personal judgment over others' opinions. He advocates for avoiding revenge, aligning with Stoic forgiveness, and suggests proactive approaches to challenges rather than starting from a disadvantage. Holiday also encourages limiting constant availability to maintain focus and tranquility, not forming opinions on everything to avoid stress, and seeking self-validation instead of external approval. Holiday's guidance reflects key Stoic virtues. His advice on embracing change and not fearing it embodies Courage, showing bravery in adapting to new situations. Not sweating the small stuff and trusting one's judgment align with Wisdom, focusing on what truly matters and making informed decisions. Avoiding revenge and not seeking external approval resonate with Justice, advocating for ethical conduct and self-reliance. The recommendations to limit availability and not have an opinion on everything demonstrate Temperance, promoting self-control and balance. In this video, the Stoic 'don'ts' offer practical steps for integrating Stoic virtues into everyday life. His approach provides a clear pathway to a more peaceful and fulfilling existence, emphasizing the importance of Stoic principles in modern living and personal development. This reflection highlights how Stoicism, through its virtues, guides us in making choices that enhance our well-being and life satisfaction.

Conclusion

Throughout these videos, a striking theme emerges: the structured presentation of Stoic principles through lists of habits, dos, and don'ts. This approach resonates with my belief that philosophy should be a practical toolkit for life, not just a theoretical exercise. Ryan Holiday’s method of distilling Stoicism into actionable steps mirrors my conviction that philosophy serves as a guide for living, offering tangible strategies for personal growth and resilience. This structured approach to Stoicism, emphasizing practical application, echoes my initial thoughts about Stoicism being a "practical philosophy." It underscores the notion that Stoicism is more than an abstract idea; it's a dynamic philosophy that can be woven into the fabric of daily life. In my journey, the virtues of Courage, Justice, Temperance, and Wisdom have been more than lofty ideals; they have become integral to my personal development and daily reflection. Each virtue, as presented and manifested in varying forms across the videos, is not just discussed but exemplified through practical habits and actions. For me, Stoicism isn't just about understanding life; it's about actively shaping it. From the bus ride to the holiday party, Stoicism and its four cardinal virtues can be applied, providing a comprehensive framework for personal development. In essence, Stoicism as a practical philosophy is about taking these ancient virtues and making them work in our real, everyday world for genuine improvement and fulfillment.
